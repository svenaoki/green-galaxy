I'm a data science architect — which is a fancy way of saying I help turn complex analytical ideas into systems that actually work in the real world. My background is in scientific computing, applied geophysics, and machine learning.

Over the years I've moved between research, consulting, and industry roles — from seismic signal processing in Paris to deep learning and AI architecture in Zürich. I enjoy connecting strong technical foundations with practical outcomes.

## What I care about

**Technical depth.** I like understanding how things work, whether it's the math inside a transformer or why a model behaves the way it does in production. Strong fundamentals make for better decisions.

**Practical impact.** A prototype is nice. A system that runs reliably and creates value is better. I aim for the latter.

**Keeping it clear.** Complexity is inevitable; jargon isn't. I try to explain things so they actually land — whether I'm talking to engineers, stakeholders, or my family at dinner.

## Outside of work

When I'm not wrangling ML pipelines, you'll find me outside — ideally somewhere mountains meet water. Ski touring in winter, trailrunning in the warmer months, and swimming in lakes whenever I can. I used to surf too, back when life had more free time.

I also run a small army of Raspberry Pis out of sheer stubbornness — because who needs an NVIDIA GPU when you have a dozen credit-card-sized computers stacked in a corner? My homelab is more "enthusiastic" than "elegant," but it keeps me happy.
